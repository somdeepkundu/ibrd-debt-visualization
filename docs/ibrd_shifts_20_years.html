A Twenty-Year Reading of IBRD Outstanding Loans

The shifting map of who borrows from the World Bank.

In 2005 the league table read like a Latin-American almanac — Brazil, Mexico, Argentina at the top, with China and Turkey climbing fast. Two decades later the geography has tilted east, and Asia now anchors the order. This is a record of who rose, who fell, and which countries quietly walked away from the world's oldest development bank.

The Climb

India moved from third to undisputed first.

In 2005, India sat behind Turkey and Brazil with a 10% share of IBRD flows. By 2015 it was the single largest borrower in the bank's 70-year history. In 2025 its outstanding balance still towers over the rest of the table — nearly double the next country.

The Departure

China is graduating out of the system.

China was 5th in 2005 with an 8% share. As GNI per capita crossed graduation thresholds, the Bank narrowed China's eligibility. New lending now skews toward green finance and pollution control — and the outstanding balance has fallen for a decade. By 2025, China sits 7th and shrinking.
